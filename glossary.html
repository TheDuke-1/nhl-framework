Glossary

Metric definitions used in the Superhuman model

← Back to Dashboard

Offense

Goal Differential Rate
Goals scored minus goals allowed per game. The simplest measure of team quality — positive means the team outscores opponents on average.
Formula: (GF - GA) / GP
Shot Quality Premium
How much better a team's expected goals percentage is compared to league average. Measures whether a team generates higher-quality chances than it allows.
Formula: xGF% - 50
Territorial Dominance (CF%)
Corsi For percentage at 5-on-5 — the share of total shot attempts a team generates. Above 50% means the team controls play more often than not.
Formula: Corsi For % at 5v5
HDCF%
High-Danger Corsi For percentage. The share of high-danger scoring chances a team generates. Widely regarded as the single best predictor of playoff success.
Formula: HD Shot Attempts For / (HD For + HD Against)
Star Power
Measures the impact of a team's best player relative to the league's top scorers. Elite stars drive playoff success disproportionately.
Formula: Top scorer PPG vs league leaders
Roster Depth
How balanced the scoring is across the lineup. Teams that rely on one line are easier to shut down in a 7-game series.
Formula: Players with 20+ goals and 40+ points

Defense & Goaltending

Goaltending Quality (GSAx)
Goals Saved Above Expected. Measures how many goals a goalie prevented compared to what an average goalie would allow given the same shots. Positive = better than average.
Formula: GSAx (Goals Saved Above Expected)
Sustainability (PDO)
The sum of a team's shooting % and save %. Tends to regress toward 100 over time. Teams with high PDO may be riding luck; teams with low PDO may be due for improvement.
Formula: PDO regression to mean (100 = sustainable)

Special Teams

Special Teams Composite
Combined power play and penalty kill effectiveness. A positive number means the team gains more from its PP than it gives up on the PK.
Formula: PP% + PK% - 100

Situational & Intangibles

Clutch Performance
How a team performs in pressure situations — close games, overtime, and when protecting leads. Teams that win tight games tend to have a mental edge in the playoffs.
Formula: One-goal wins + OT wins - Blown leads
Road Performance
Ability to win away from home. Critical for the playoffs, where half the games are on the road regardless of seeding.
Formula: Road win % vs league average
Recent Form
How a team has played in the last 10-20 games compared to its season average. Captures late-season surges and slumps.
Formula: Points % in recent games vs season average
Playoff Experience
The team's recent playoff history and success. Teams with deep runs in recent years tend to handle playoff pressure better.
Formula: Weighted playoff rounds won (last 5 years)
Dynasty Score
Recent championship pedigree. Captures the "championship DNA" factor — teams that have won recently have institutional knowledge of what it takes.
Formula: Recency-weighted Cup wins and Finals appearances

Model Metrics

Composite Strength
The model's overall team power rating. A weighted average of all 14 performance metrics, calibrated against 15 years of historical data. Higher = stronger team.
Formula: Weighted average of 14 performance metrics
Vegas Cup Signal
The market's consensus on each team's Cup chances, derived from betting odds. The model uses this as one input — markets aggregate information efficiently but aren't perfect.
Formula: Implied probability from betting odds
Playoff Probability
The model's estimated chance that a team makes the playoffs. Based on current standings, remaining schedule strength, and statistical projections.
Monte Carlo simulation over remaining schedule
Cup Probability
The model's estimated chance that a team wins the Stanley Cup. Derived from 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations of the full playoff bracket.
Cup wins / total simulations
Tier
Teams are grouped into four tiers based on composite strength: Elite (clear Cup contenders), Contender (strong playoff teams), Bubble (could go either way), and Longshot (unlikely but not impossible).
Assigned by model based on composite strength clustering
WAR (Wins Above Replacement)
An estimate of how many additional wins a player contributes compared to a replacement-level player. Used in the injury column to quantify the impact of missing players.
Estimated from position, ice time, and production
Edge (Betting)
The difference between the model's Cup probability and the implied probability from betting odds. A positive edge suggests the market is undervaluing the team.
Formula: Model probability - Implied probability from odds
